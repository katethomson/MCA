Advantage of Time Frequency Analysis

An advantage of time frequency analysis is the ability to identify when new elements occur more easily. For example, in track three, the introduction of the deep bass element, or at least the introduction of something fairly major, is easily identifiable by the orange lines. The same change or addition is not as easily identifiable in waveform. the representation of track one, most of the instruments are introduced as one and change does not occur much throughout the track. We can see this continuity in the spectogram however this is also assumed by analysing the waveform. Track two, like track three, introduces elements gradually which is reflected better in the spectrogram than in waveform. In track two, the first few seconds are so high pitched and quiet, it is hard to identify any activity in the waveform, however it is obvious there is some type of activity in the spectogram.
